# Sample Agent Profile

Use this as an example of how to fill `ai-assistant/memory/agent-profile.md`.

## Identity

- Company or project name: SignalForge
- Product name: SignalForge OS
- Industry: B2B SaaS for product and go-to-market teams
- Business model: Subscription SaaS
- Stage: Early growth

## Audience

- Primary user: Product managers and startup founders
- Buyer: Founder, head of product, or small leadership team
- Secondary users: Designers, engineers, growth leads
- Geography or region: English-speaking global market

## Product context

- Core problem: Small teams have fragmented product, GTM, and execution workflows with poor decision quality and weak cross-functional alignment.
- Main workflow: Capture idea -> clarify problem -> shape spec -> review UX and risks -> plan delivery -> launch and iterate.
- Key differentiator: One AI collaborator that combines product, architecture, bug-finding, security, GTM, and model-selection thinking in one operating system.
- Main competitors: ChatGPT custom setups, Claude project workflows, Notion AI workflows, generic startup copilots, consulting-heavy product advisory services.

## Operating preferences

- Preferred posture: Skeptical, pragmatic, high-signal, decision-oriented
- Risk tolerance: Moderate; willing to move fast, but not with silent logic or security gaps
- Product priorities: Clarity, execution quality, trust, and practical usefulness
- Delivery constraints: Small team, limited bandwidth, need for reusable workflows over bespoke process
- Brand or tone notes: Sharp, direct, useful, non-hype

## Research notes

- Stable market truths worth preserving: Founders want speed, but trust and decision quality matter once outputs affect roadmap, code, or GTM decisions.
- Security or compliance obligations: Avoid accidental exposure of internal strategy, code, or customer data in shared prompts or examples.
- UX quality bar: Clean, high-signal, minimal fluff, fast-to-scan outputs
- Planning cadence: Weekly execution, monthly review, quarterly positioning updates
- Marketing channels to prioritize: Content, founder-led distribution, community, high-intent SEO, and partner amplification
